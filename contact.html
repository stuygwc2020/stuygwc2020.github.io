About Us

Stuyvesant’s Girls Who Code chapter is part of the national non-profit organization working to close the gender gap in technology. Through this club, we aim to educate, equip, and inspire girls with the computing and technical skills they’ll need to pursue computer science.

Although the statistics at Stuy are slightly better than nationwide statistics, there is still a huge decrease in the retention rate for females progressing from introductory CS courses to AP CS. Research has shown that effective mentorship early on for aspiring female computer scientists can significantly impact their desire to stay in the field. The main goal of this club is to provide that mentorship for females pursuing computer science for the first time.

We host after school workshops to teach girls about computer science topics and skills beyond the classroom, such as competitive computing or data analytics, arrange Q and A sessions, and organize field trips to large tech companies like Google or Buzzfeed. We also frequently invite successful women in the tech industry to come to Stuy and speak at our meetings. Upperclassmen serve as student leaders to provide a supportive environment that encourages girls to continue pursuing computer science. It is our goal to make sure that every girl walks away from a Girls Who Code meeting having gained some of the confidence they need to succeed in a male-dominated industry.

Girls Who Code is unique because we are one of few clubs that uses learning and academics to address social inequality. We believe that exposure early on is the key to higher retention rates for females in computer science. Although there are many other computer science clubs, our organization is one of few that uses our platform as a school club to address larger social issues.
